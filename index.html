Memento Mori

Bring me a cup of tea..

I am glad you exist. “You gave meaning to my life,” he said in a distant voice. His words resonated through the room, and the walls were not accustomed to hearing such things. Until that moment, it had all felt like a staged play.

“Be careful of people who say they are trying to save your soul. That is your job,” he told me, tilting his head as if he wanted to move away from someone—or something. I didn’t fully understand what he meant.

After that, he spoke with a voice that seemed to belong to another person, or perhaps another time. I knew he would never repeat those words again.

“Stay away from people who label you, call you names, or pronounce your name funny. Your happiness is your responsibility. Take care of your thoughts; let them nurture your soul. Your body is your temple. Your true home is not a house, nor a country. I made my home in art and interactions.”

He paused, then continued: “Nurture this moment. Remember that grief is also a part of life. Your soul is your home. Your emotions serve a purpose: they guide you. If they insult you, tailor them to suit your soul. Your gut feeling is the closest thing to an angel you’ll find. There is a holy being who should look after you—that holy being is you.”

He shifted his distant gaze from the horizon towards me, only to see if I understood him clearly and looked back out the window.

He took another sip of the tea and a defiant smile emerged.
